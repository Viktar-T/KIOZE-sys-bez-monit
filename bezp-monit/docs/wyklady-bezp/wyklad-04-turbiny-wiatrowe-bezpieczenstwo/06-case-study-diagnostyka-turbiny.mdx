<Slide title="🎯 Case study: diagnostyka turbiny wiatrowej" type="info">

<LearningObjective>
Po tej sekcji student potrafi zastosować kompleksową analizę diagnostyczną turbiny wiatrowej, łączyć dane z różnych źródeł monitoringu, podejmować decyzje operacyjne na podstawie analiz CMS oraz planować działania prewencyjne i naprawcze.
</LearningObjective>

<KeyPoints title="📋 Scenariusz case study">
- 🏭 **Farma wiatrowa**: 25 turbin 3MW w centralnej Polsce
- 🔧 **Problem**: Degradacja systemu przekładni w turbinie T12
- 📊 **Dane**: 18-miesięczny monitoring CMS + awaryjne zatrzymania
- 🎯 **Cel**: Analiza przyczyn, decyzje operacyjne, planowanie napraw
</KeyPoints>

<SupportingDetails title="🔧 Specyfikacja turbiny T12">
**Parametry techniczne**:
- **Model**: Vestas V115-3.0 MW
- **Średnica wirnika**: 115m, 3 łopaty
- **Wysokość wieży**: 93m steel tower
- **Przekładnia**: Planetary + helical gear, ratio 1:104
- **Generator**: Permanent magnet synchronous, direct drive variant z przekładnią
- **CMS**: Siemens SCM System, 54 kanałów wibracji, 25 temperatur, olej online

**Historia eksploatacji**:
- **Commissioning**: Czerwiec 2019
- **Pierwsza awaria**: Lipiec 2020 (12 miesięcy eksploatacji)
- **Druga awaria**: Luty 2021 (20 miesięcy eksploatacji)  
- **Trzecia awaria**: Sierpień 2021 (26 miesięcy eksploatacji)
- **Analiza**: Wrzesień 2021 (po trzeciej awarii)
</SupportingDetails>

<SupportingDetails title="📈 Charakterystyka problemu - początkowy opis">
**Pierwsza awaria** (Lipiec 2020):
- **Typ**: Nieoczekiwane zatrzymanie przy wietrze 18 m/s
- **Alarm**: "High vibration planetary gearbox"
- **Czas naprawy**: 3 tygodnie
- **Skutek**: Wymiana łożyska planetary carrier
- **Koszt**: €45,000

**Druga awaria** (Luty 2021):
- **Typ**: Alarm "Gearbox oil temperature high"
- **Warunki**: Wiatr 22 m/s, temperatura otoczenia -15°C
- **Akcja**: Awaryjne zatrzymanie, wymiana filtra oleju
- **Koszt**: €8,000

**Trzecia awaria** (Sierpień 2021):
- **Typ**: "Planetary gear vibration alarm"
- **Warunki**: Normalne warunki operacyjne (12 m/s)
- **Status**: Turbina offline, wymagająca analizy CMS
- **Problem**: Czy to powtarzający się problem, czy nowa awaria?
</SupportingDetails>

<WarningBox title="⚠️ Kontekst biznesowy problemu">
**Konsekwencje dla farmy**:
- **Utrata przychodów**: 3MW × €80/MWh × 24h/dzień = €5,760/dzień offline
- **Koszty napraw**: Łącznie €53,000 w 2 lata (vs. planowe maintenance €15,000)
- **Ryzyko**: Pattern failure może dotyczyć innych turbin w farmie
- **Decyzja**: Wymiana całej przekładni vs. naprawa progressive?
</WarningBox>

<Slide title="📊 Analiza danych CMS - 18-miesięczna historia" type="info">

<KeyPoints title="🔍 Główne obszary analizy">
- 🌊 **Analiza wibracji** - spektra, trendy, envelope analysis
- 🌡️ **Monitoring temperatury** - przekładnia, łożyska, olej
- 🛢️ **Analiza oleju** - skład, zanieczyszczenia, degradacja addytywów
- 📈 **Analiza trendów** - correlation analysis, pattern recognition
</KeyPoints>

<SupportingDetails title="🌊 Analiza wibracji - planetary gearbox">
**Trend analysis (18 miesięcy)**:

**RMS vibration trends**:
```
Month 1-6:  Stable at 3.2-3.5 mm/s
Month 7-12: Gradual increase to 4.1 mm/s
Month 13-18: Rapid increase to 6.8 mm/s (alarm threshold: 6.0 mm/s)
```

**Spectral analysis changes**:
```
Baseline (Month 1-6):
- Planetary carrier frequency (PCF): 1.1 mm/s
- Gear mesh frequency (GMF): 2.2 mm/s  
- Sidebands: Minimal, poniżej 0.3 mm/s

Degradation phase (Month 13-18):
- PCF: 1.1 → 2.8 mm/s (+155%)
- GMF: 2.2 → 4.1 mm/s (+86%)
- Sidebands around GMF: 0.3 → 1.4 mm/s (+367%)
- New frequency components: 2×PCF, 3×PCF (modulation)
```

**Envelope analysis**:
```
High-frequency envelope (2-12 kHz):
- Month 1-6: Stable baseline 0.8 mm/s
- Month 13-18: Progressive increase to 2.1 mm/s
- Pattern: Sporadic spikes developing into continuous elevated levels
```
</SupportingDetails>

<SupportingDetails title="🌡️ Analiza temperatury - correlation analysis">
**Temperature correlation with loads**:

**Normal correlation** (Month 1-12):
```
Gearbox temp = 45°C + (Power_MW × 8°C/MW)
R² = 0.89 (good correlation)
```

**Degraded correlation** (Month 13-18):
```
Gearbox temp = 58°C + (Power_MW × 12°C/MW)
R² = 0.72 (degraded correlation)
Excess temperature: +13°C regardless of load
```

**Detailed temperature analysis**:
```
Planetary carrier bearing:
- Normal: 55-65°C (seasonal variation)
- Month 16: 75°C average (+20°C anomaly)
- Month 18: 85°C pre-failure level

Oil temperature correlation:
- Gearbox temp vs Oil temp: Strong correlation until Month 14
- Month 15-18: Gearbox heating faster than oil circulation can handle
```

**Critical finding**: Heat buildup indicates lubrication problems
</SupportingDetails>

<Example title="Oil analysis - progressive degradation pattern">
**Chemiczne analizy oleju** (co 3 miesiące):

**Physical properties trend**:
```
Viscosity (40°C):
Month 6:  220 cSt (nominal)
Month 9:  218 cSt (stable)
Month 12: 235 cSt (+7% - first alarm)
Month 15: 268 cSt (+22% - alarm level exceeded)
Month 18: 312 cSt (+42% - critical level)

TBN (Total Base Number):
Month 6:  8.2 mg KOH/g (fresh oil baseline)
Month 12: 6.1 mg KOH/g (acceptable degradation)
Month 15: 3.8 mg KOH/g (alarm threshold)
Month 18: 2.2 mg KOH/g (critical - oil failing)
```

**Wear metals analysis**:
```
Iron (Fe) - primary wear indicator:
Month 6:  12 ppm (normal)
Month 9:  18 ppm (+50%)
Month 12: 35 ppm (+192%)
Month 15: 68 ppm (+467%)
Month 18: 125 ppm (+942% - critical failure level)

Copper (Cu) - bearing/carrying material:
Month 6:  8 ppm
Month 18: 42 ppm (+425% - bearing wear confirmed)

Chromium (Cr) - hardened steel components:
Month 6:  5 ppm
Month 18: 28 ppm (+460% - gear wear confirmed)
```

**Particle count (ISO 4406)**:
```
Month 6:  18/16/13 (clean oil)
Month 12: 20/17/14 (slight degradation)
Month 15: 22/19/16 (contamination increasing)
Month 18: 26/23/19 (critical contamination level)

Size distribution analysis:
- >14 μm particles: 15x increase
- >6 μm particles: 8x increase
- Pattern: Indicates severe wear, not just contamination
```
</Example>

<KeyConcept title="Root cause analysis - integrated findings">
**Synthesis of all CMS data** reveals **progressive gear mesh degradation**:

**Timeline of deterioration**:
1. **Month 6-9**: Early gear mesh wear detectable only in envelope analysis
2. **Month 9-12**: Vibration sidebands appear, oil viscosity starts increasing  
3. **Month 12-15**: Temperature correlation degrades, metal particles escalate
4. **Month 15-18**: All parameters exceed alarm levels, failure imminent

**Primary failure mechanism**: **Micro-pitting in planetary gear teeth**
- Caused by: Insufficient lubrication under high loads
- Accelerated by: Misalignment or load distribution problems
- Confirmed by: Progressive metal particles (Cr) and vibration sidebands
</KeyConcept>

<SupportingDetails title="⚙️ Immediate actions (pierwsze 2 tygodnie)">
**Stabilizacja sytuacji**:
```
Week 1:
- Emergency oil change with premium filter (€3,000)
- Load reduction to 85% maximum power
- Daily vibration and temperature monitoring
- Contract signed for planetary gear kit (€65,000)

Week 2:
- Enhanced oil analysis setup (weekly sampling)
- Vibration baseline recording at reduced load
- Preparation of replacement timeline
- Communication with turbine operator about power reduction
```

**Krótkoterminowe cele**:
- **Stabilize temperatures**: Target poniżej 70°C gearbox temperature
- **Monitor vibration**: Ensure levels don't exceed 7.0 mm/s threshold
- **Oil quality**: Prevent further TBN degradation
- **Safety**: Avoid unexpected shutdown during lead time for parts
</SupportingDetails>

<SupportingDetails title="📅 Implementation timeline (4-miesięczny plan)">
**Month 1 - Preparation phase**:
```
- Parts procurement confirmation
- Technical team coordination (Vestas + local contractor)
- Preparation of special tools and crane equipment
- Risk assessment and safety procedures
```

**Month 2 - Low wind season preparation**:
```
- Weather window identification (January - typically low wind)
- Mobilization of replacement team
- Pre-replacement vibration and oil baseline
- Final parts delivery and quality check
```

**Month 3 - Implementation (January)**:
```
Week 1: Turbine shutdown, gearbox removal, inspection
Week 2: Planetary gear replacement, quality control
Week 3: Reinstallation, commissioning, initial testing
Week 4: Performance verification, baseline establishment
```

**Month 4 - Post-implementation monitoring**:
```
- Daily monitoring for first 2 weeks
- Weekly comprehensive analysis (vibration + oil + temperature)
- Performance comparison with farm average
- Documentation and lessons learned
```
</SupportingDetails>

<Example title="Post-repair monitoring protocol - 6-miesięczny plan">
**Monitoring intensywność** (pierwszych 6 miesięcy post-repair):

**Daily monitoring** (tylko pierwszy miesiąc):
```
Morning checklist:
- Vibration levels: All planetary frequencies
- Temperature: Gearbox vs. farm average
- Power output: Efficiency comparison
- Alarm status: Any new warnings?
```

**Weekly comprehensive analysis**:
```
Vibration spectrum analysis:
- PCF (Planetary Carrier Frequency): Target poniżej 1.5 mm/s
- GMF (Gear Mesh Frequency): Target poniżej 2.5 mm/s  
- Sidebands: Should be minimal poniżej 0.5 mm/s
- Overall RMS: Target poniżej 4.0 mm/s

Oil analysis (first 3 months monthly, then quarterly):
- Physical properties: Viscosity, TBN
- Wear metals: Fe, Cu, Cr levels
- Particle count: ISO 4406 classification
```

**Monthly reporting**:
```
Performance metrics:
- Availability: Compare with pre-repair (target: >97%)
- Power efficiency: vs. nameplate and farm average
- Maintenance costs: Track any follow-up issues

Trend analysis:
- Are all parameters stable and improving?
- Early warning detection: Any degradation patterns?
- Comparison with other farm turbines for validation
```

**Success criteria** (6-month assessment):
```
Technical success:
- Vibration levels within normal range for all planetary frequencies
- Temperature correlation restored (R² >0.85)
- Oil quality maintained without rapid degradation
- Zero unexpected shutdowns

Business success:
- Availability restored to >97% (vs. 94.2% pre-repair)
- ROI calculation: Repair cost vs. avoided revenue loss
- Lessons learned documented for other turbines in farm
```
</Example>

- **Vibration levels**: All parameters within normal ranges
- **Temperature stability**: Consistent correlation with load
- **Oil quality**: Stable TBN, minimal metal content increase
- **Availability**: >97% (vs. 94.2% pre-repair)

**Long-term expectations** (5+ year outlook):
- **Gearbox life**: 10+ additional years expected

<KeyConcept title="Lessons learned and preventive strategy">
**Wnioski dla całej farmy**:

**Early detection opportunities**:
- **Month 6-9**: Envelope analysis showed early signs (could have triggered investigation)
- **Month 12**: Oil viscosity increase + vibration sidebands (could have planned repair)
- **Month 15**: Multiple parameters exceeded thresholds (should have initiated action)

**Improved maintenance strategy**:
- **Enhanced oil monitoring**: Bi-monthly analysis for all turbines
- **Vibration envelope**: Monthly envelope analysis for planetary gearboxes
- **Temperature trending**: Automated correlation analysis with load
- **Cross-turbine comparison**: Benchmark individual turbines against farm average

**Preventive measures for other turbines**:
- **Load optimization**: Avoid excessive loads during high wind periods
- **Oil quality**: Premium oil with enhanced additive packages
- **Monitoring frequency**: Increased CMS analysis frequency for aging turbines
</KeyConcept>
